Product: BreedLog
Scope: Professionally add pedigree, bloodline, genetic-line, breeding-system, mating-risk, and genetic-performance support to BreedLog without breaking existing animal records, exports, offline use, or normal user workspace separation.

Objective:
BreedLog must now treat genetics as a first-class livestock-management layer. The app must clearly separate breed, pedigree, bloodline, genetic line, breeding system, and measured performance. This module must support Meatmaster stud breeding, commercial meat production, replacement selection, mating planning, relationship-risk warnings, and professional animal exports.

Core terminology:

1. Breed = the animal’s breed or breed type.
2. Pedigree = the actual ancestry chain: sire, dam, grandparents, and further generations where available.
3. Bloodline = a named family/source line, such as Baksteen, Bosch, Zelenski, Rolo, or Nitro. A bloodline is not automatically superior; its value must be proven by recorded performance.
4. Genetic line = a managed selection direction inside the flock, such as maternal ewe line, terminal meat line, stud replacement line, or veld-performance line.
5. Breeding system = the mating strategy being used: pure-breeding, out-breeding, inbreeding, linebreeding, crossbreeding, terminal crossing, rotational crossing, roto-terminal crossing, grading up, or composite breeding.
6. Performance = the recorded proof that a line is working: fertility, lamb survival, growth, weaning, structure, adaptability, progeny output, cull rate, and retained replacements.

Mating Planner relationship checks:
When a ram and ewe are selected for mating, BreedLog must scan the available pedigree and classify the relationship risk.

Required classifications:

* Critical inbreeding risk: parent-offspring, full siblings, or same animal selected on both sides.
* High relationship risk: half-siblings, grandparent-grandchild, uncle/aunt to niece/nephew.
* Managed linebreeding: shared desired ancestor but not extremely close.
* Outbreeding: same breed/type with no common ancestor within 4–6 recorded generations.
* Crossbreeding: different breed or breed composition/type.
* Unknown risk: pedigree too incomplete to judge safely.

The UI must explain the result in plain language and must never silently approve a risky mating.
